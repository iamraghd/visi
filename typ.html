Rocky Planets

Rocky planets, also referred to as terrestrial planets, are predominantly composed of solid materials, such as silicates and metals. These planets are characterized by their solid surfaces and well-defined structural layers, which include the outer crust, the mantle, and the inner core. Within our solar system, this category encompasses Earth, Mercury, Venus, and Mars.

Gas Giants

In contrast, gas giants are primarily composed of gaseous substances and do not possess a solid surface, distinguishing them from their rocky counterparts. These planets are largely made up of hydrogen and helium, with minor proportions of other gases. They lack a distinct surface and exhibit a gradual transition from gaseous outer layers to a denser metallic core. Notable examples of gas giants in our solar system include Jupiter and Saturn..
