The Mystery of Black Holes Black holes are dense cosmic objects with gravity so strong that nothing, not even light, can escape their event horizon. Scientists detect them through their effects on nearby matter, such as glowing accretion disks and star orbits, revealing their hidden presence. Contrary to popular belief, they aren't wormholes or vacuum cleaners, but massive objects with gravitational pulls like any other..

White Holes: The Cosmic Opposites White holes are theoretical regions in space where nothing can enter, contrasting with black holes, from which nothing escapes. They emerge from the same equations of general relativity, suggesting a fascinating link between the two. While black holes trap matter, white holes allow it to escape, challenging our understanding of time and causality..
